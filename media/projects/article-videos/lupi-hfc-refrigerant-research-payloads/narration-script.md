# HFC Refrigerant Research Payloads — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Calm, precise, moderate pace. Treat the refrigerant problem as a computational screening bottleneck, not a policy summary. Land the contrast between global warming potential and molecular scale. Let the payload and correction layers feel like tools the viewer can trust.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — The refrigerant dilemma · 0:00–0:16

**Narration:** Hydrofluorocarbons were introduced to protect the ozone layer, but their warming potential is hundreds to thousands of times that of carbon dioxide. The Kigali Amendment now aims to cut HFC consumption roughly eighty percent by 2047, which could avoid up to half a degree Celsius of warming by 2100.

**Visual cue:** A single refrigerant molecule diffuses into a glowing global temperature curve; a counter resolves on **HUNDREDS TO THOUSANDS × CO₂** and the Kigali target **~80% BY 2047**.

### Beat 2 — Why replacements are hard · 0:16–0:31

**Narration:** Finding replacements is hard because the design space is constrained by thermophysical performance, flammability, toxicity, atmospheric lifetime, and lubricant compatibility all at once. Computational screening must predict vapor pressure, latent heat, transport properties, and decomposition pathways across millions of small molecules.

**Visual cue:** A multi-axis constraint diagram—performance, flammability, toxicity, lifetime, lubricant compatibility—tightens around a shrinking candidate cloud.

### Beat 3 — The under-coordination problem · 0:31–0:46

**Narration:** Each of those properties depends on under-coordinated environments—radical transition states, surfaces, and phase boundaries—where generic force fields and universal machine-learning potentials systematically soften the energy surface. That softening can invert rankings and send labs chasing the wrong molecule.

**Visual cue:** A smooth bulk lattice opens into a surface and transition state; the coordination axis falls while an indigo error band widens into a **SOFTENING** warning.

### Beat 4 — Inspectable trajectories · 0:46–1:02

**Narration:** The new research payloads turn molecular trajectories into inspectable datasets. They carry full per-atom charges, velocities, forces, potential and kinetic energy, plus thermo tables and temperature-profile sidecars, all streamed in sync with the simulation. For refrigerants, that means watching the temperature profile evolve and correlating forces with local structure.

**Visual cue:** A trajectory frame dissolves into layered data panels—charges, velocities, forces, thermo, temperature profile—orbiting a refrigerant liquid snapshot.

### Beat 5 — Glyphs and profiles in lockstep · 1:02–1:19

**Narration:** Vector glyphs render per-atom force, velocity, and dipole arrows as camera-facing ribbon impostors, with p95 auto-scaling and magnitude colormaps. A fix ave/chunk parser replays spatial temperature, density, and velocity profiles in lockstep with the trajectory—the view transport-studies researchers actually need.

**Visual cue:** A refrigerant box fills with colored velocity arrows and a synchronized temperature-profile strip scrolling alongside the trajectory scrubber.

### Beat 6 — Correction and verification · 1:19–1:35

**Narration:** Inspectable trajectories feed directly into the correction-and-verification layer. The correction recovers trustworthy barriers and lifetimes in under-coordinated environments, while the verification layer separates supported predictions from synthesis-dependent ones. Screening stops chasing false winners and starts sending laboratories candidates they can trust.

**Visual cue:** A pipeline flows from raw trajectory through a correction field into a proof gate; claims receive **SUPPORTED** and **SYNTHESIS-DEPENDENT** stamps, and a short experimental queue brightens.
