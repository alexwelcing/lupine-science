## Required line edits

### 1. Beat 1, source attribution and denominator clarity

**Current (line 11):**
> Okay, so AI predicted three hundred eighty thousand stable new materials. Huge, right? But by late 2023, only seven hundred thirty-six had actually been synthesized. That’s zero point two percent.

**Replace with:**
> Okay, so Google DeepMind’s GNoME identified three hundred eighty thousand computationally stable materials. Huge, right? But by late 2023, only seven hundred thirty-six had been independently synthesized. That’s about zero point two percent.

**Reason:** “AI predicted” is needlessly vague; naming GNoME makes the statistic auditable. “Computationally stable” is the precise category in the source article, “independently” is a material qualifier, and “about” avoids implying more precision than the rounded percentage supports.

### 2. Beat 7, avoid claiming the gap is already closed

**Current (line 53):**
> The goal is simple: predictions that flow to synthesis, characterization, and scale—not another generation cycle. Read the full article to see how we close the zero-point-two-percent gap.

**Replace with:**
> The goal is simple: predictions that flow to synthesis, characterization, and scale—not another generation cycle. Read the full article to see why closing the zero-point-two-percent gap requires correction and verification.

**Reason:** The article diagnoses the gap and argues for the required layer; it does not present evidence that Lupine has already closed the full synthesis gap. The revised CTA is ambitious but supportable.
