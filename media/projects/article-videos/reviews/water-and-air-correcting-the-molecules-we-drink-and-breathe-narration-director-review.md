## Director assessment

The seven-beat structure is strong and visually legible. It moves cleanly from human stakes to the shared low-coordination failure mode, makes the water and air applications concrete, and closes on one memorable platform thesis. The tone is mostly right for Lupine: calm, precise, urgent without becoming alarmist.

Narration approval is withheld for two scientific-scope errors in the proof beat and one overbroad setup claim. The script currently turns evidence from published uMLIP tests and Lupine’s fcc-metal surface-energy study into demonstrated corrected performance across water and air materials. That transfer is the prospectus; it is not what the cited blind test measured. Two local pacing issues also need correction before TTS.

## Required line edits

### P0 — Beat 2: do not claim the models’ “biggest errors” across all target chemistries

**Current (line 27):**
> Here is the catch: the AI models screening those materials make their biggest errors exactly where the chemistry happens—in tiny pores, exposed metal centers, and single-atom sites. These atoms have fewer neighbors, or lower coordination, than the bulk crystals in the training data.

**Replace with:**
> Here is the catch: the models screening these materials can make large errors at low-coordination sites—tiny pores, exposed metal centers, and single atoms. Those environments are poorly represented by the bulk crystals common in training data.

**Reason:** “Biggest errors exactly where the chemistry happens” is a universal comparative claim not established by the cited evidence. The replacement preserves the causal setup while distinguishing an evidenced risk from a measured maximum across every water and air chemistry.

### P0 — Beat 3: scope the 15–60% result to the published tests

**Current (line 33):**
> In that low-coordination zone, universal machine-learning potentials can soften the energy landscape by 15 to 60 percent. Barriers look too easy. Binding looks wrong. A raw screen can rank the flashy failure above the material that actually works.

**Replace with:**
> In published tests, universal machine-learning potentials systematically softened surface and defect energetics—sometimes by 15 to 60 percent. Barriers can look too easy. Binding can shift. A raw screen can rank an apparent winner above the material that actually works.

**Reason:** The current line presents 15–60% as a quantified result for the water and air application spaces. The cited systematic-softening study establishes the failure mode in tested properties and pathways, not a measured 15–60% error for every membrane, sorbent, or catalyst named here. “Binding looks wrong” is also too categorical; “can shift” is accurate and still conversational.

### P0 — Beat 6: state what the blind test actually predicted

**Current (line 51):**
> Lupine measures that coordination error, adds analytic correction forces at runtime, and verifies where the answer is supported. Across 36 blind model-and-material combinations, corrected predictions reached a correlation of zero point nine zero six—with zero adjustable parameters—while staying about one hundred thousand times faster than DFT.

**Replace with:**
> Lupine measures the coordination error and adds analytic correction forces at runtime. In an fcc-metal blind test, the measured field predicted a never-fitted surface-energy error across 36 model-and-material cells, reaching a correlation of zero point nine zero six with zero adjustable parameters. Universal potentials remain roughly one hundred thousand times faster than DFT.

**Reason:** The r = 0.906 result is a blind prediction of the never-fitted γ110 surface-energy error across 36 fcc-metal model/material cells. It is not a correlation for corrected water and air predictions. The speed figure compares the uMLIP class with DFT; it was not established by that correlation test as a benchmark for corrected water and air workflows. The replacement keeps both proof points but prevents them from being fused into a broader validation claim.

**Required visual correction (line 53):** Replace `CORRECTED PREDICTIONS · r = 0.906` or any equivalent generic framing with `BLIND ERROR PREDICTION · FCC METALS · r = 0.906`. Keep `0 ADJUSTABLE PARAMETERS`. Show `~100,000× VS DFT` as a separate class-level speed comparison, not as part of the scatter-plot result.

### P1 — Beat 1: leave room for the opening evidence to register

**Current (line 21):**

**Replace with:**
> Two billion people live with water scarcity. Air pollution is linked to millions of premature deaths each year. Better materials could help: membranes that reject salt, sorbents that harvest water from dry air, and catalysts that destroy pollutants.

**Reason:** The current 41 words consume the full 14-second window at 175.7 WPM with no room to land either statistic. The 38-word replacement reads at about 163 WPM and changes “depends on” to the more defensible “could help.”

## Accuracy notes

- **Two billion people / millions of premature deaths:** faithful to the article’s deliberately rounded opening claims. “Linked to” is appropriately cautious.
- **Low coordination at pores, exposed centers, and single-atom sites:** scientifically coherent and faithful to the article, but should be framed as the shared risk/hypothesis rather than a completed validation across all listed chemistries.
- **15–60% softening:** retain only with explicit tested-evidence scope.
- **Water and air examples:** desalination pores, atmospheric-water hydrolysis, Li/Mg separation, cold-start NOx, soot oxidation, and VOC oxidation are faithful compressions of the article’s application map.
- **r = 0.906 / 36 cells / zero adjustable parameters:** supported only when identified as blind prediction of a never-fitted fcc-metal surface-energy error. Do not describe it as corrected predictions across water and air materials.
- **~100,000× DFT:** acceptable as an approximate uMLIP-versus-DFT class comparison, not as a measured outcome of the 36-cell blind test.
- **Verification language:** “verifies where the answer is supported” is too broad unless attached to defined claims and applicability boundaries. The required replacement avoids implying that an entire water or air prediction has been formally verified.
