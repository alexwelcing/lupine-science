# From Fantasy Frameworks to Makeable Materials — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Calm, precise, moderate pace. Treat the synthesis gap as the central tension. Land the contrast between "millions generated" and "can it be made." Let "certificate," "flywheel," and "worth making" carry the closing weight. No urgency; let the formalism feel inevitable.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — Hook · 0:00–0:15

**Narration:** Generative AI can now invent millions of metal-organic frameworks at machine speed. What it cannot yet do is promise that any of them can actually be made. In the first half of 2026, that gap has become the central bottleneck in materials AI.

**Visual cue:** A generative model pours out countless wireframe MOF lattices; a narrow filter lets only one certified structure pass while the rest dissolve. Title resolves: **FANTASY FRAMEWORKS → MAKEABLE MATERIALS**.

### Beat 2 — The synthesis gap · 0:15–0:30

**Narration:** A structure that is stable on a computer screen may be kinetically inaccessible, chemically incompatible with real solvents, or so strained that it collapses the moment it is removed from the simulation box. Until that changes, most AI-generated MOFs remain fantasy frameworks.

**Visual cue:** A perfect digital MOF lattice on a monitor crumbles into fragments at the touch of a solvent droplet; the split screen reads **SIMULATED** versus **ISOLATED**.

### Beat 3 — The certificate · 0:30–0:48

**Narration:** The proposed fix is a makeability layer encoded in the Lean 4 proof assistant. It turns the rules of assembly, stability, and synthesis into formal theorems. The output is not a prediction. It is a certificate: a machine-checkable argument that a candidate satisfies explicitly stated assumptions for a given synthesis protocol.

**Visual cue:** Metal nodes and organic linkers snap into a validated assembly; a formal proof ribbon wraps the structure and seals it with a **CERTIFICATE** stamp.

### Beat 4 — The flywheel · 0:48–1:05

**Narration:** Tethered to a real lab, the method becomes a self-improving flywheel. Formal predicates filter candidate structures. Only certified candidates reach synthesis. Both success and failure are logged formally. Each failed synthesis tightens the certificate and rules out an entire class of future candidates, making the next cycle faster.

**Visual cue:** A circular four-stage flywheel—**FORMALIZE → SIMULATE → SYNTHESIZE → FEEDBACK**—spins as a single bright candidate moves through while failed candidates are pulled into a rule-update loop.

### Beat 5 — Starting point · 1:05–1:20

**Narration:** The starting point is intentionally narrow: over the first twelve to eighteen months, focus on one MOF family, one synthesis modality, one stability stressor, one database, and one closed-loop pilot. That discipline reflects a track record of eighty-five formally proven lemmas and zero remaining sorry axioms in the existing theory.

**Visual cue:** A narrow pipeline with five labeled chambers—**FAMILY · MODALITY · STRESSOR · DATABASE · PILOT**—next to a proof ledger reading **85 LEMMAS · 0 GAPS**.

### Beat 6 — Thesis · 1:20–1:35

**Narration:** The next frontier in materials discovery is not generating more structures. It is proving which ones are worth making. The verification layer gives materials scientists a reason to trust a candidate before they spend time, money, and reagents on the bench.

**Visual cue:** A laboratory bench with one bright certified framework in the foreground; countless gray unverified structures fade into the background. Closing card: **PROVE WHAT IS WORTH MAKING**.
