# A Field, Not a Neural Net — Storyboard

This video tells materials engineers and climate-tech investors that systematic uMLIP errors can be measured as a coordination-dependent field, corrected without another neural net, and bounded by machine-checked proof.

The narration below matches the current narration script verbatim. Each sentence is mapped to a timed visual state inside one of seven continuous frame worlds; sentence-level states transform in place rather than creating a slide for every line.

## Direction

- **Thesis:** Wrongness at under-coordinated atomic environments is structured, measurable, correctable, and formally bounded.
- **Rhythm:** shock–collapse–diagnose–reveal–BUILD–prove–expand–resolve.
- **Emotional journey:** Open with the scale gap and a skeptical question; replace frustration with the pleasure of seeing hidden geometry; peak on the blind correlation and Lean rejection; close with disciplined optimism rather than hype.
- **Evidence peak:** Beat 4 — the never-fitted (110) surface error correlates with measurement at **r = 0.906 across n = 36**.
- **Transition plan:** Four of six scene handoffs use the 12-frame indigo-line wipe (66.7%); the field itself earns one 15-frame field-line reveal; one closely related correction-to-proof handoff uses the 12-frame subtle push + crossfade. Beat 7 resolves through the 21-frame paper fade inside its final state so the canonical mark holds for the final two seconds.
- **Audio notes:** Fast, bright 165–180 WPM delivery over a restrained low paper/room-tone bed at least 12 dB below narration. Use only evidence-linked SFX: dry count ticks, a soft contour draw, three anchor clicks, a low proof-kernel refusal, and one quiet resolving chime. Micro-pause after “Huge,” after “It has a shape,” and before the corrected count.
- **Composition rule:** Every world uses paper + ink + indigo and no more than one semantic accent. Newsreader carries the argument; IBM Plex Mono carries values, axes, provenance, and proof states. Critical visuals remain above the caption reserve.
