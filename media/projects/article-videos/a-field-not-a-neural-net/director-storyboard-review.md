## Decision rationale

The storyboard turns the approved narration into a clear seven-world argument: scale gap → under-coordination → measured error geometry → blind evidence → runtime correction → proof boundary → climate payoff. Each world advances the thesis rather than illustrating the voiceover literally, and the strongest evidence lands at the right moment: the never-fitted `(110)` result and `r = 0.906 · n = 36` form the visual peak before the correction and proof consequences.

The direction is strongly aligned with Lupine Science. It uses the canonical paper/ink/indigo system, assigns Newsreader to claims and IBM Plex Mono to evidence, treats proof as a visible claim lifecycle, and avoids generic neural-network, stock-crystal, and climate-icon imagery. The visual density comes from real scientific structure and provenance chrome rather than UI cards.

Motion ambition is sufficient for animation. The field is not merely drawn: residuals organize, coordination probes move causally along the spline, a blind region is revealed in sequence, energy streams combine additively, analytic forces emerge from the derivative, and the proof kernel visibly refuses a false inequality. The transition plan is disciplined and matches the frame system rather than becoming transition roulette.
