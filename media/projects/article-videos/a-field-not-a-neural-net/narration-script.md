# A Field, Not a Neural Net — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Keep it bright, quick, and conversational—not salesy. Land the contrast words (“not random,” “measured,” “blind,” and “say no”). Give the proof result a clean micro-pause, then build momentum into the climate examples.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — Hook · 0:00–0:13

**Narration:** Okay, so Google DeepMind predicted 2.2 million crystals. Huge. But by late 2023, only 736 had been independently synthesized. The usual response is: build a bigger neural net. What if that is solving the wrong problem?

**Visual cue:** A wide prediction funnel counts rapidly to **2.2M**, then collapses to **736 synthesized**. The title question replaces the funnel: **BIGGER MODEL—or WRONG ERROR MODEL?**

### Beat 2 — The real problem · 0:13–0:29

**Narration:** Universal machine-learning potentials are already fast and pretty accurate for tidy bulk crystals. But real materials work at vacancies, surfaces, and transition states—places where atoms have fewer neighbors. There, errors jump, and ion-migration barriers can be underestimated by more than 60 percent.

**Visual cue:** An ordered bulk lattice peels open into a surface, vacancy, and hopping transition state. A coordination axis falls from **12** toward **7** while the signed-error curve rises smoothly; highlight **>60% barrier underestimate**.

### Beat 3 — The field · 0:29–0:44

**Narration:** Here is the twist: that wrongness is not random. It has a shape. Lupine measures an environment error field based on coordination number—basically, how many neighboring atoms each atom has. No second neural net. No per-system retraining.

**Visual cue:** Scattered error marks organize into one smooth indigo field line. Neighbor counts pulse around representative atoms; comparison labels lock in: **MEASURED FIELD** versus **ANOTHER TRAINING LOOP**.

### Beat 4 — Three anchors, one blind test · 0:44–1:01

**Narration:** Three standard measurements anchor the curve: two surface energies and one vacancy energy. Bulk coordination is fixed at zero error. Then the field predicts a fourth surface—the 110 facet—blind. Across 36 model-and-material combinations, predicted and measured errors correlate at 0.906.

**Visual cue:** Plot three amber knots at coordination **8, 9, and 11**, pin **P(12) = 0**, then extend into the shaded blind region at **7**. Transition to a predicted-versus-measured scatter plot; draw the fit and resolve **r = 0.906 · n = 36**.

### Beat 5 — Runtime correction · 1:01–1:17

**Narration:** Flip that field, add it beside the existing model, and under-coordinated structures move back toward reference energies. The forces are analytic, so simulations stay physically consistent. In tests, nickel surface error dropped from 9.7 to 1.5 percent, while bulk lattice constants stayed unchanged.

**Visual cue:** A compact uMLIP block feeds energy and force arrows through an additive correction layer. Before-and-after bars shrink **Ni: 9.7% → 1.5%** as the bulk lattice remains registered and unchanged.

### Beat 6 — Proof that can say no · 1:17–1:34

**Narration:** And this layer does something most AI pipelines cannot: it can say no. Lean 4 proofs check every quantitative claim and flag ranking inversions, already-converged cases, or physics outside the field’s domain. One proof even caught a rounding tie and corrected “27 improvements” to 26.

**Visual cue:** A machine-check ribbon stamps claims **PROVED**, **REFUSED**, or **OUT OF DOMAIN**. Zoom into the rejected inequality **813 < 813**, cross out **27**, and replace it with **26 of 36**.

### Beat 7 — Payoff · 1:34–1:46

**Narration:** That matters for battery ion hops, carbon-capture frameworks, ammonia catalysts, and perovskite stability—all controlled by under-coordinated defects. So the goal is not more confident predictions. It is fast predictions with measured corrections, certified boundaries, and a provable reason to stop.

**Visual cue:** Four climate-material motifs orbit the same coordination-defect geometry, then collapse into the closing thesis: **MEASURE THE ERROR. CORRECT THE FIELD. PROVE THE BOUNDARY.** End on the Lupine Science mark and article title.
