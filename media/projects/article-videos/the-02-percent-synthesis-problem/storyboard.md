# The 0.2% Synthesis Problem — Storyboard

This video tells technical materials and climate-tech audiences that generating stable crystals is not the bottleneck; correcting and verifying their path to synthesis is. The approved narration is reproduced verbatim below. Article claims and source IDs refer to [`../../../../articles/the-02-percent-synthesis-problem.md`](../../../../articles/the-02-percent-synthesis-problem.md).

## Direction

- **Thesis:** The 0.2% validation rate is a structural handoff failure, and Lupine’s environment error field is the correction layer that can move credible candidates from prediction to furnace to scale.
- **Rhythm:** rush–drop–build–shock–resolve–expand–land. The opening compresses scale into one arresting ratio; the four-filter sequence gets the longest explanatory build; the 100 meV / 50× inversion is the evidence peak; the error field resolves the mechanism before the climate stakes expand the frame and the CTA lands quietly.
- **Emotional journey:** Start with astonishment, turn it into productive skepticism, make the hidden failure modes feel inspectable, peak on the conductor/insulator inversion, then move from technical confidence to climate urgency and a clear next step.
- **Evidence peak:** Beat 04 — a 100 meV barrier miss produces an approximately 50× hopping-rate change at room temperature and can flip the material classification.
- **Visual doctrine:** Rebuild the prototype concepts as native, editable vector/data scenes; the existing JPEGs under `../prototype-01-the-02-percent-synthesis-problem/images/` are composition references only because production frames must not contain baked-in text. Use the approved reusable `title-card`, `data-chart`, `lower-third`, and `outro` compositions where their contracts fit.
- **Transition plan:** Six handoffs: four 12-frame indigo-line wipes (66.7%), one 15-frame field-line reveal at the correction turn, and one 21-frame paper fade into the outro. The evidence trace must resolve into an axis, gate, contour, or rule in the incoming scene; no separate exit animation.
- **Audio notes:** Fast, conversational female narration at the approved 165–180 WPM performance target, with intentional micro-pauses after “Huge, right?”, “zero point two percent,” “This is not a tiny accuracy problem,” and “The goal is simple.” Bed: restrained tactile pulse at least 12 dB below narration. SFX are sparse and evidentiary: tile rush/collapse, four gate locks, barrier click + rate acceleration, furnace cutoff, deployment clock, and final pipeline lock.
- **Caption plan:** Captions use the dedicated x 192–1728 / y 828–954 zone. All charts, labels, and decisive marks remain above y 810; captions never cover evidence.
