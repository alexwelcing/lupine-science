# Why Lupine Science? — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Calm, precise, moderate pace. Treat "makeability" as the central idea and land it quietly. Avoid hype; let the structure of the argument carry the weight. End with an open invitation, not a sales close.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — Fantasy frameworks · 0:00–0:17

**Narration:** Artificial intelligence can invent millions of new materials in an afternoon. But inventing a structure is not the same as inventing a thing. Most generated candidates are fantasy frameworks: beautiful on a screen, unstable in a flask. The bottleneck is no longer imagination. It is makeability.

**Visual cue:** A generative model pours out countless crystal structures; most dissolve or collapse, leaving only a few solid candidates. Title resolves: **PREDICTION ≠ MAKEABILITY**.

### Beat 2 — The error geometry · 0:17–0:34

**Narration:** The simulations are wrong in structured ways. Their errors cluster on thin, low-dimensional manifolds of failure. That structure is the opening: predictable failure is correctable failure. If you can characterize the error, you can constrain the search. If you can constrain the search, you can promise makeability.

**Visual cue:** Scatter points of error organize into one smooth indigo manifold curve. Labels lock in: **CHARACTERIZE → CONSTRAIN → PROMISE**.

### Beat 3 — Certificates · 0:34–0:51

**Narration:** The method treats the error manifold as a first-class object. Its shape is measured. Properties about it are proved. And those properties fold back into the generative model. The result is not another black-box score. It is a certificate: a machine-checkable argument for synthesis, stability, and performance.

**Visual cue:** A measured curve passes through a proof gate, then feeds back into the generative model. The output is a stamped certificate rather than a star rating.

### Beat 4 — Three-part system · 0:51–1:11

**Narration:** The makeability layer has three parts. First, formal rules encode what synthesizable, stable, and useful actually mean, living in a proof assistant so they can be audited and extended. Second, simulations run only over the region of design space the rules allow. Third, every experiment updates the rules, so each failed synthesis makes the next certificate stronger.

**Visual cue:** Three connected blocks—**RULES · SIMULATE · VALIDATE**—forming a closed loop. A failed synthesis feeds back as a stronger rule.

### Beat 5 — Why now · 1:11–1:31

**Narration:** Three forces make this timing real. Generative models for molecules and crystals are now fast and cheap enough to flood the design space. Machine-learning potentials such as MACE and Orb are accurate enough to replace expensive quantum screening. And proof assistants such as Lean 4 are mature enough to express and verify non-trivial mathematical physics.

**Visual cue:** Three converging streams—generative models, machine-learning potentials, proof assistants—meet at a single **MAKEABLE DISCOVERY** node.

### Beat 6 — Protocol and invitation · 1:31–1:45

**Narration:** The offering is a protocol, not a model: a way for labs, model builders, and formalizers to collaborate on materials that can actually be made. The record is open. The theorems are being written. The materials are waiting.

**Visual cue:** A shared workspace connects lab, model, and proof terminals. Closing text: **OPEN RECORD · MACHINE-CHECKED THEOREMS · MAKEABLE MATERIALS**.
