# Cement, Concrete, and the Weight of the Built World — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Calm, precise, moderate pace. Land the scale numbers (2.8 gigatonnes, 8%, 60%) with quiet weight. Treat cement as a materials-discovery problem, not a commodity footnote. End on the platform implication.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — Scale · 0:00–0:14

**Narration:** Concrete is the most widely used manufactured material on Earth. The cement that binds it emits about 2.8 gigatonnes of CO₂ every year, roughly 8% of global anthropogenic emissions. And about 60% of that comes from limestone calcination, not fuel.

**Visual cue:** A rotating globe wrapped in concrete infrastructure; a counter locks on **2.8 GtCO₂/YR** and a pie chart splits emissions into **60% PROCESS · 40% FUEL**.

### Beat 2 — The process-emissions trap · 0:14–0:28

**Narration:** Clean kilns help, but they cannot change the chemistry. As long as limestone is the main calcium feedstock, calcination releases CO₂ regardless of heat source. Real decarbonization needs new chemistries: alternative binders, alternative clinkers, and CO₂-cured concrete.

**Visual cue:** A kiln cross-section shows fuel flames and limestone decomposing into CaO plus a CO₂ cloud labeled **INTRINSIC**.

### Beat 3 — Why computation struggles · 0:28–0:45

**Narration:** All three routes depend on amorphous gels, metastable hydrates, and multi-component oxides. These are the exact environments where universal machine-learning potentials soften the energy surface by 15 to 60 percent. A dissolution energy off by 15 percent can invert the ranking of two binder formulations.

**Visual cue:** An ordered crystal lattice dissolves into a disordered gel; the coordination axis drops and a red error band widens to **15–60% SOFTENING**.

### Beat 4 — Three discovery fronts · 0:45–1:02

**Narration:** Alternative binders like slag and geopolymers are glasses and gels, not unit cells. Alternative clinkers span multi-component oxide spaces with metastable essential phases. CO₂-cured concrete is limited by carbonate diffusion barriers at under-coordinated surface sites. Each front needs trustworthy rankings.

**Visual cue:** Three vertical panels—**BINDERS**, **CLINKERS**, **CO₂-CURED**—each highlights an under-coordinated atomic environment and a corrected ranking arrow.

### Beat 5 — Measure, correct, prove · 1:02–1:18

**Narration:** The correction measures error as a field over local coordination, anchored to three observables and pinned to zero in bulk. It applies analytic corrections at runtime, keeping molecular dynamics nearly as fast as the base model. Across 36 blind model-material pairs, corrected predictions correlate at 0.906 with zero fitted parameters.

**Visual cue:** A smooth indigo field curve anchored by three amber knots; a pipeline flows **uMLIP → CORRECTION → ANALYTIC FORCES** with metric **r = 0.906 · n = 36**.

### Beat 6 — Verification as a guardrail · 1:18–1:32

**Narration:** The verification layer uses build-locked Lean 4 theorems to separate supported predictions from synthesis-dependent ones. For cement, that matters because the most useful phases are metastable and cannot be judged by convex-hull stability alone.

**Visual cue:** A proof ribbon stamps claims **SUPPORTED**, **BOUNDED**, or **SYNTHESIS-DEPENDENT**; a metastable hydrate phase moves from red discard to amber bounded.

### Beat 7 — Platform thesis · 1:32–1:48

**Narration:** Cement is not a special case. It is one instance of a geometry of wrongness that extends across batteries, catalysts, sorbents, and refrigerants. The bottleneck is not a shortage of candidates. It is a shortage of predictions laboratories can trust.

**Visual cue:** Seven environmental domains orbit the same coordination-defect geometry, then collapse to the closing thesis: **MEASURE · CORRECT · PROVE**.
