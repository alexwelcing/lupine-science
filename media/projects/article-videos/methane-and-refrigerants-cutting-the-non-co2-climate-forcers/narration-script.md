# Methane and Refrigerants: Cutting the Non-CO₂ Climate Forcers — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Start with clean urgency and make the two climate levers feel surprisingly connected. Keep the application montage nimble and curious. Slow slightly for “fifteen to sixty percent,” the correction pipeline, and the final claim about trust.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — The climate levers beyond carbon · 0:00–0:15

**Narration:** Carbon dioxide gets the headlines. But cutting methane thirty percent by 2030 could avoid about zero point three degrees of warming by 2040. Phasing down HFC refrigerants could avoid up to another zero point five degrees by 2100. That is huge near-term leverage.

**Visual cue:** A CO₂ headline wall slides aside to reveal two rising levers: **METHANE · 0.3 °C** and **HFCs · UP TO 0.5 °C**. Policy markers snap to **2030** and **2100**.

### Beat 2 — Two targets, one missing-material problem · 0:15–0:29

**Narration:** Here is the twist: both targets depend on materials we do not have yet. We need catalysts that convert stranded methane without burning the product, and refrigerants that are efficient, safe, and low-warming—all at once.

**Visual cue:** Split screen: a remote methane plume enters a catalyst and becomes liquid methanol; an air conditioner cycles through a three-point gauge labeled **EFFICIENCY · SAFETY · LOW GWP**. Both panels stop at **MATERIAL MISSING**.

### Beat 3 — Where fast models fail · 0:29–0:44

**Narration:** Fast atomistic AI should help search the options. Except its biggest errors happen exactly where these materials work: surfaces, transition states, radical fragments, and phase boundaries. In those under-coordinated environments, universal models can soften the energy landscape by fifteen to sixty percent—and rank the wrong candidate first.

**Visual cue:** Zoom from an orderly bulk crystal into four glowing edge environments. An energy barrier sags **15–60%**; candidate cards reshuffle into a false ranking.

### Beat 4 — Methane’s chemical trap · 0:44–0:59

**Narration:** Methane’s carbon-hydrogen bond is brutally strong. A catalyst active enough to crack it often holds the methanol product too tightly and over-oxidizes it into carbon dioxide. Corrected barriers can expose rare sites that break this trap—and open lower-temperature methanol or cleaner hydrogen routes.

**Visual cue:** A reaction pathway forks: the raw path races from **CH₄** past **CH₃OH** to **CO₂**; the corrected path releases methanol. A second branch yields hydrogen plus solid carbon.

### Beat 5 — Refrigerants without the ugly compromise · 0:59–1:14

**Narration:** Refrigerants face a different-looking version of the same problem. Millions of molecules must be screened for vapor pressure, efficiency, flammability, toxicity, and atmospheric lifetime. The safety-critical steps involve radicals and breaking bonds—the same low-coordination physics that raw models misread.

**Visual cue:** A molecular search grid races through millions of candidates. Filters stack rapidly—**PRESSURE, EFFICIENCY, FLAMMABILITY, TOXICITY, LIFETIME**—then a radical transition state glows amber.

### Beat 6 — Measure, correct, verify · 1:14–1:35

**Narration:** Lupine measures the error versus atomic coordination, anchors a correction field, and applies it at runtime—without retraining the base model. Then machine-checked proofs mark which predictions stay inside the measured domain. Across thirty-six blind model-and-material combinations, corrected predictions reached a correlation of zero point nine zero six, with zero adjustable parameters.

**Visual cue:** Pipeline assembles: **MEASURE → CORRECT AT RUNTIME → VERIFY**. Three anchor points pull a curve into place. A blind scatter plot locks to the unity line: **n = 36 · r = 0.906 · 0 FITTED PARAMETERS**.

### Beat 7 — One shared correction geometry · 1:35–1:46

**Narration:** Methane catalysts and next-generation cooling are not separate discovery stories. They share one hidden error geometry—and one chance to turn fast screening into evidence we can actually trust, before the policy clock runs out.

**Visual cue:** Methane conversion, molecular refrigerants, and solid-state cooling orbit one luminous correction field. End card: **ONE ERROR GEOMETRY · TWO CLIMATE LEVERS · VERIFIED DISCOVERY**.
