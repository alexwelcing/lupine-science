# Investing in the Trust Layer — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Move fast, but let the proof numbers land. Keep the opening lightly incredulous, the three-pillar section crisp, and the investment case grounded rather than promotional. Emphasize “believe,” “say no,” and “trust layer.”

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — The scarce resource · 0:00–0:13

**Narration:** AI can generate millions of possible materials, simulate them in microseconds, and send robots to synthesize them around the clock. So what is scarce now? Not ideas. Not compute. The scarce resource is believing the result.

**Visual cue:** Crystal candidates flood across a dark field; counters race upward beside a simulation clock and robotic lab arm. Everything freezes on one amber word: **BELIEVE?**

### Beat 2 — The trust bottleneck · 0:13–0:27

**Narration:** Google DeepMind reported 380,000 stable crystal predictions. By late 2023, only 736 had been independently synthesized. That is zero point two percent. Every false positive wastes lab weeks. Every false negative can bury a climate material we needed yesterday.

**Visual cue:** A funnel plunges from **380,000 STABLE** to **736 SYNTHESIZED · 0.2%**. Rejected cards split into **WASTED EXPERIMENT** and **MISSED MATERIAL**.

### Beat 3 — Where Lupine sits · 0:27–0:42

**Narration:** Lupine sits between prediction and synthesis—not replacing generators or simulators, but checking their work. It measures systematic model error, corrects that error at runtime, and proves which claims a laboratory can actually trust.

**Visual cue:** Pipeline assembles: **GENERATE → PREDICT → LUPINE: CORRECT + VERIFY → SYNTHESIZE**. A noisy candidate card passes through Lupine and exits with corrected values and a proof seal.

### Beat 4 — Three defensible pillars · 0:42–1:04

**Narration:** The moat has three parts. First: a measured error field, anchored by standard observables—not another neural net that needs retraining for every material. Second: 77 build-locked Lean 4 theorems, with zero unproven shortcuts. Third: a LAMMPS runtime overlay that works beside tools like CHGNet and MACE. Today, it adds 15.6 percent overhead. Compiled, the target is below one percent.

**Visual cue:** Three vertical panels snap in: **MEASURED FIELD**, **77 THEOREMS · 0 “SORRY”**, and **RUNTIME OVERLAY**. A gauge moves from **+15.6% PYTHON** toward **<1% COMPILED TARGET**.

### Beat 5 — Proof must say no · 1:04–1:19

**Narration:** And the proof layer is not decorative. Lean once rejected a claim that passed statistical filtering, changing 27 successes to 26. That is the product in miniature: trustworthy infrastructure has to catch its own mistakes—and say no when the evidence does not hold.

**Visual cue:** A green **27 OF 36** stamp hits a theorem checker, flashes red, and resolves to **26 OF 36 · VERIFIED**. Unsupported claims peel away from the lab queue.

### Beat 6 — Compounding value, honest risk · 1:19–1:39

**Narration:** Each screening campaign adds measured bias data. Each experiment tightens the next prediction. Each theorem expands the verified boundary. What is proven today is the field for face-centered-cubic metals. Extending it to other crystal families—and securing industrial validation—is still work ahead. That honesty is part of the moat.

**Visual cue:** A flywheel connects **FIELD DATA → PROOF LIBRARY → EXPERIMENT → BETTER SCREENING**. Proven **FCC** glows solid; **BCC**, **HCP**, **LAYERED**, and **PARTNER VALIDATION** appear as clearly labeled next-stage gates.

### Beat 7 — The investment thesis · 1:39–1:53

**Narration:** So this is not a bet on one magic material. It is a bet on the trust layer that makes discovery more capital-efficient: fewer predictions, better defended, reaching the lab before the climate window closes.

**Visual cue:** Millions of dim candidates condense into five bright, proof-backed climate-material cards moving into real labs. End card: **INVEST IN THE TRUST LAYER** · **LUPINE SCIENCE** · article URL or QR.
