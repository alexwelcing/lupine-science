# Director Script Review — Critical Minerals, PFAS, and the Remediation Imperative

**Decision:** REVISIONS REQUESTED

**Reviewed:** `narration-script.md`

## Executive note

The seven-beat arc is strong and distinctly Lupine: evidence-led, conversational, technically specific, and ambitious without drifting into disaster-trailer language. The critical-minerals/PFAS parallel lands clearly, and the close is memorable. Approval is withheld for one factual-precision issue and a beat-level timing mismatch.

## Required line notes

### P0 — Beat 3, narration line 25: qualify the four-nanogram threshold

Current:

> First, capture molecules at just four nanograms per liter.

Issue: four nanograms per liter is the U.S. EPA maximum contaminant level for **PFOA and PFOS individually**, not a generic capture threshold for all PFAS. As written, “molecules” overgeneralizes the regulation.

Requested revision:

> First, capture PFOA and PFOS down to the EPA limit: four nanograms per liter each.

Keep the next sentence about destruction. The correction preserves the urgency while making the claim auditable against article footnote 2.

## Advisory notes (not approval blockers)

- Beat 4’s “fifteen to sixty percent” claim tracks the source article, but **“soften the potential-energy surface”** is more precise than the broader “misrepresent the energy landscape.” The current wording is acceptable for narration.
- Beat 5 appropriately avoids voicing the visual’s `~100,000× DFT` shorthand. If that number is later voiced, preserve the article’s qualified wording: “roughly five orders of magnitude faster than DFT.”
- Beat 6’s verification claim is directionally consistent with the article. Keep **“flag”** rather than escalating it to a claim that every unsupported synthesis outcome is experimentally proved impossible.
- Pronunciation note “PEE-fass” is suitable for the selected conversational voice; use it consistently in TTS and captions.
