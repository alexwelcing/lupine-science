# Five Materials That Could Unlock 5–12 GtCO₂/Year — Storyboard

This video tells materials engineers and climate-tech investors that five independently grounded climate-material targets are not five unrelated bets: each depends on under-coordinated atomic environments where fast universal models fail, and each can benefit from the same measured correction and verification layer.

The approved narration is reproduced verbatim below. Every sentence maps to a timed visual state inside one of seven continuous frame worlds. Timing follows silence boundaries measured from the 92.376-second approved master `narration-ana-final.wav`, not the provisional ranges in the script. Claims and source keys trace to [`../../../../articles/five-materials-for-5-to-12-gtco2-year.md`](../../../../articles/five-materials-for-5-to-12-gtco2-year.md).

## Direction

- **Thesis:** The portfolio’s climate leverage comes from a shared computational bottleneck: defect-mediated properties can be screened credibly only when systematic energy error is corrected and unsupported predictions are refused.
- **Rhythm:** reveal–expand–question / step–step–build–build–peak / COLLAPSE–diagnose–invert / correct–sort–refuse / resolve. The five-target run accelerates without becoming a listicle; “Different industries, one shared failure” creates the structural drop; the 100 meV → ~50× inversion is the evidence peak; refusal is the emotional apex; the close is calm and conditional.
- **Emotional journey:** Begin with the surprise that climate targets depend on missing matter, let the portfolio feel broad and tangible, then collapse that breadth into one inspectable atomic failure. Replace scale anxiety with disciplined confidence: measure, correct, prove, and know when to stop.
- **Evidence peak:** World 05 / line 14 — a 100 meV barrier error changes a predicted hopping rate by approximately 50× at room temperature and can invert candidate rankings.
- **Visual doctrine:** Treat each material as a real mechanism, not a stock icon: transition-metal migration in an LMR lattice; Li⁺ hopping through a solid electrolyte; sparse CO₂ capture at MOF pores under humid flow; N₂ activation at an under-coordinated catalyst site; Sn-vacancy oxidation in a perovskite lattice. Rebuild evidence as native vectors/data. Article JPEGs are concept references only; never mount their baked text as production evidence.
- **Composition:** Shared warm paper, ink, and indigo. Use one semantic accent at most: amber for risk/heat, sage for supported correction, or rose for refusal. Newsreader carries claims; IBM Plex Mono carries measurements, axes, target numbers, and provenance. Keep decisive geometry above y=810 to reserve x 192–1728 / y 828–954 for captions.
- **Transition plan:** Six world handoffs: four 12-frame indigo-line wipes (66.7%), one 15-frame field-line reveal at the shared-failure turn, and one 21-frame paper fade into the final outro. Every wipe resolves into a real axis, pore channel, reaction coordinate, or correction trace in the incoming world; no pre-transition exit animation.
- **Audio notes:** Preserve the master’s pauses after “usually a material,” “every year,” each numbered target, “one shared failure,” and before the closing line. Bed: low tactile paper pulse at least 12 dB below narration. SFX stay evidentiary: five target locks, ion-hop ticks, humidity deflection, reactor pressure release, oxidation clock stop, barrier/ranking relay, proof refusal, and one resolving hardware lock.
