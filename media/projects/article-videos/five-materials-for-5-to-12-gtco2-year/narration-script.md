# Five Materials That Could Unlock 5–12 GtCO₂/Year — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Move quickly, but let each material land. The opening should feel surprising, the five-item run should build momentum, and “one shared failure” is the turn. Keep the technical language crisp and curious, never breathless or promotional.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — Hook · 0:00–0:12

**Narration:** The gap between a climate target and an actual climate solution is usually a material. And five missing materials could unlock between five and twelve gigatonnes of carbon dioxide reductions every year. So, what are they?

**Visual cue:** A global emissions counter climbs, then five material silhouettes snap into a horizontal lineup. Resolve the headline figure: **5 MATERIALS · 5–12 GtCO₂/YEAR**.

### Beat 2 — Better batteries · 0:12–0:32

**Narration:** First, cobalt-free cathodes that push past a thousand charge cycles without voltage fade. Second, solid electrolytes made from abundant elements, so lithium-metal batteries can pass four hundred watt-hours per kilogram without flammable liquid or critical-material pricing.

**Visual cue:** A conventional battery splits into two upgrades. On the cathode side, cobalt fades out as the cycle counter passes **1,000**. On the electrolyte side, liquid disappears and a solid-state cell rises past **400 Wh/kg**.

### Beat 3 — Pulling carbon from air · 0:32–0:47

**Narration:** Third, metal-organic frameworks for direct air capture. They need to grab carbon dioxide at just four hundred parts per million, ignore all that competing water, survive humidity, and stay cheap enough to manufacture by the tonne.

**Visual cue:** A porous MOF lattice catches sparse CO₂ molecules from humid air. Water droplets bounce away; gauges lock on **400 ppm**, **40–70% RH**, and **<$50/kg**.

### Beat 4 — Cleaner fertilizer and safer solar · 0:47–1:06

**Narration:** Fourth, an ammonia catalyst that works near room conditions instead of inside the energy-hungry Haber-Bosch process. And fifth, a lead-free perovskite solar absorber that can clear twenty percent efficiency and last twenty-five years without tin oxidizing in minutes.

**Visual cue:** A hot, pressurized ammonia reactor shrinks into a renewable-powered electrochemical cell. Match-cut to a perovskite module: **Pb** exits, **Sn** enters, then an oxidation clock stops as **>20% · 25 YEARS** appears.

### Beat 5 — One shared failure · 1:06–1:24

**Narration:** Different industries, one shared failure. Performance depends on defects, surfaces, and atomic transition states where atoms have fewer neighbors. That is exactly where fast universal models soften the energy landscape and get the ranking wrong. Brute-force quantum calculations can recover the answer, but they are far too slow for millions or trillions of candidates. A one-hundred-milli-electronvolt error can change a predicted hopping rate fifty-fold.

**Visual cue:** The five materials collapse onto the same under-coordinated atomic site. An energy barrier sags; candidate rankings flip. Highlight **100 meV ERROR → ~50× RATE CHANGE**.

### Beat 6 — Correct, verify, refuse · 1:24–1:41

**Narration:** Lupine measures that systematic error as a physical field, corrects it while the model runs, and checks the result with machine-verified proofs. Good candidates move up. Bad ones fall away. And when a prediction leaves the field’s measured domain, the system can refuse to pretend.

**Visual cue:** A translucent correction field lifts the sagging barrier back into place. Candidate cards reorder, then receive **CORRECTED**, **PROVED**, or **OUT OF DOMAIN** stamps.

### Beat 7 — Payoff · 1:41–1:48

**Narration:** Five materials, one correction layer, and a path from plausible crystals to climate hardware that can actually scale.

**Visual cue:** Five corrected crystal structures flow into a battery, carbon-capture unit, ammonia cell, and solar module. End card: **CORRECT THE ERROR. PROVE THE BOUNDARY. BUILD WHAT SCALES.**
