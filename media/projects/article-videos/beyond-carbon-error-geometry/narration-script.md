# Beyond Carbon: The Error Geometry of Environmental Materials — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Start with lightly incredulous momentum, then turn precise on the atomic explanation. Keep the seven-domain sweep energetic rather than list-like. Let “not climate-specific,” “has a shape,” and “prove the boundary” land. Confident, never salesy.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — One error, seven crises · 0:00–0:13

**Narration:** What do desalination membranes, methane catalysts, safer refrigerants, lithium recovery, PFAS cleanup, clean-air catalysts, and low-carbon cement have in common? Weirdly, the same atomic blind spot. The materials bottleneck is not climate-specific. It is coordination-specific.

**Visual cue:** Seven environmental icons orbit a perfect crystal. The crystal fractures into one shared glowing defect labeled **UNDER-COORDINATION**; title resolves: **ONE ERROR GEOMETRY · SEVEN PLANETARY BOUNDARIES**.

### Beat 2 — Where models go soft · 0:13–0:29

**Narration:** Universal machine-learning potentials learn tidy bulk crystals, where atoms have lots of regular neighbors. But useful chemistry happens at pores, surfaces, vacancies, and transition states—where atoms have fewer neighbors. Right there, these models can soften the energy landscape by 15 to 60 percent.

**Visual cue:** Ordered bulk lattice at **CN 12** opens into pore, surface, vacancy, and transition-state close-ups at **CN 4–8**. A curve rises into a shaded **15–60% SOFTENING** band as coordination falls.

### Beat 3 — Error becomes bad priorities · 0:29–0:44

**Narration:** That is not harmless noise. A softened barrier can make the wrong membrane look selective, the wrong catalyst look fast, or an unmakeable cement phase look stable. Across millions of candidates, tiny systematic errors flip rankings—and experiments chase false winners.

**Visual cue:** Three raw-model leaderboards—membrane, catalyst, cement—shuffle dramatically after correction. Red false winners drop while one buried green candidate rises to **EXPERIMENTAL PRIORITY**.

### Beat 4 — Planetary stakes · 0:44–1:02

**Narration:** The stakes are huge: water scarcity affects roughly two billion people. Air pollution kills millions every year. Methane drives about 30 percent of current warming. Clean-energy mineral demand could rise four- to six-fold by 2040. And cement alone produces roughly 2.8 gigatonnes of carbon dioxide annually.

**Visual cue:** Fast radial dashboard: **2B WATER**, **MILLIONS OF DEATHS**, **30% OF WARMING**, **4–6× MINERALS**, **2.8 Gt CO₂/YR**. All spokes reconnect to the same under-coordinated atomic site.

### Beat 5 — Measure the shape · 1:02–1:17

**Narration:** Lupine’s response starts with the useful part: the error has a shape. Three measured observables anchor an environment error field. Bulk coordination is fixed at zero error, and a constrained curve predicts corrections for under-coordinated environments the field never directly saw.

**Visual cue:** Three amber anchor points lock onto a coordination curve; **BULK · CN 12 · ZERO ERROR** pins the endpoint. The curve extends into an unseen region and reveals a corrected energy value.

### Beat 6 — Correct fast, prove honestly · 1:17–1:36

**Narration:** Add that field at runtime, and simulations follow corrected energies and analytic forces—still about one hundred thousand times faster than quantum chemistry. In blind tests across 36 model-material pairs, predicted corrections correlated at 0.906. Then 77 build-locked Lean 4 theorems mark what is supported, out of domain, or synthesis-dependent.

**Visual cue:** Pipeline builds: **uMLIP → ERROR FIELD → ANALYTIC FORCES → PROOF GATE**. Metric cards snap in: **~10⁵× DFT SPEED**, **r = 0.906 · n = 36**, **77 LEAN 4 THEOREMS**. Claims receive **SUPPORTED**, **OUT OF DOMAIN**, or **SYNTHESIS-DEPENDENT** seals.

### Beat 7 — The platform thesis · 1:36–1:48

**Narration:** So this is bigger than carbon. One correction-and-verification layer can improve discovery across water, air, methane, refrigerants, minerals, PFAS, and cement. Measure the error. Correct the physics. Prove the boundary. Then send experiments the candidates actually worth making.

**Visual cue:** The corrected pipeline fans into seven real-world labs, then converges on a short experimental queue of bright candidates. End card: **MEASURE · CORRECT · PROVE** · **LUPINE SCIENCE**.
