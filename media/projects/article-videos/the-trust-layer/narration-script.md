# The Trust Layer — Narration Script

## Performance direction

Calm, precise, moderate pace. Land the shift from "imagination is free" to "belief is the bottleneck" with quiet weight. Avoid any company-centric framing; speak as the method itself. End on the invitation to check the open record.

## Beat sheet

### Beat 1 — The oldest loop · 0:00–0:12

**Narration:** Science works one way: guess how the world might be, test the guess against reality, keep what survives. For four centuries, reality was the speed limit. Experiments took months; synthesis took years.

**Visual cue:** A simple loop diagram—**GUESS → TEST → KEEP**—slowly rotating, with a heavy pendulum marking the long wait for reality.

### Beat 2 — Software speed · 0:12–0:25

**Narration:** Then two-thirds of that loop went to software speed. Generative models propose specific atoms in crystallographic detail. Machine-learned simulators screen faster than any lab. Imagination and cheap testing stopped being the bottleneck. Belief did not.

**Visual cue:** The loop accelerates: a crystal generator pours candidate structures into a fast screening pipeline, but the final gate is narrow and staffed by a single uncertain figure.

### Beat 3 — The bottleneck · 0:25–0:38

**Narration:** A model can be confident and wrong. The number that matters is not raw output; it is how many proposals are worth a year of lab time. That question now gates the whole pipeline. Belief is the bottleneck.

**Visual cue:** A confident prediction glows bright, then flickers and dims; a small queue of vetted candidates passes through while a mountain of raw outputs piles up behind it.

### Beat 4 — Trust as infrastructure · 0:38–0:52

**Narration:** The fix is not another disclaimer. It is a mathematical characterization of how a class of predictions fails, proved and machine-checked end to end, published with the data. A verified result is checked once, then everyone builds on it, like a proved theorem.

**Visual cue:** A proof ribbon wraps around a prediction, sealing it; downstream builders stack blocks on the seal without re-deriving it. Title resolves: **TRUST AS INFRASTRUCTURE**.

### Beat 5 — Evidence before claim · 0:52–1:07

**Narration:** The claims are modest because the standard is the product. The error geometry is formalized in Lean. The benchmark recomputes live in the browser from committed files. The rendered crystal is real published crystallography. When internal ideas fail, the failure stays in the record.

**Visual cue:** Four evidence panels lock into place: **LEAN FORMALIZATION**, **LIVE BENCHMARK**, **REAL CRYSTAL FILE**, **FAILED IDEAS KEPT**. Each panel carries a small checkmark.

### Beat 6 — Open verification · 1:07–1:23

**Narration:** A proprietary trust score would be easier to sell and impossible to believe. So the evidence ledger, proofs, and benchmark data are public. The verification layer sits between model builders and laboratories, turning confident output into shortlists whose failure modes were characterized before synthesis starts.

**Visual cue:** A black box is crossed out; an open ledger bridges a model tower and a lab bench. Shortlisted candidates carry small flags: **SUPPORTED**, **BOUNDED**, **SYNTHESIS-DEPENDENT**.

### Beat 7 — The invitation · 1:23–1:37

**Narration:** The materials that define the next century exist only on paper today. The question is whether the result is believable enough to try. The machinery that answers with proof is being built in public, so the answer never has to be produced twice.

**Visual cue:** A sheaf of predicted material blueprints floats in space; one bright candidate receives a proof seal and moves toward a real synthesis vessel. End card: **CHECK THE RECORD. THAT IS THE POINT.**
