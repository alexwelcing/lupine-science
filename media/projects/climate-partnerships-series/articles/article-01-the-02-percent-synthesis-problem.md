# The 0.2% Synthesis Problem

> **Type:** article
>
> **Date:** 2026-07-16
>
> **Scope:** The structural gap between computational prediction and experimental validation in climate-critical materials.
>
> **Description:** Why the energy transition is bottlenecked not by a shortage of predicted materials, but by the fact that only 0.2% of them have ever been made.
>
> **Audience:** Investors, materials scientists, AI-for-science teams, and the curious public
>
> **Status:** Draft

<p class="lead">The world will spend trillions on clean energy this decade, but every pathway to net-zero depends on materials that do not yet exist at commercial scale. The immediate problem is not imagination: it is validation.</p>

---

## The climate investment gap

Body: IEA numbers on batteries linked to ~20% of required 2030 CO₂ reductions and indirectly to another 40%; clean energy investment must grow from $1.8T to $4.5T annually by the early 2030s; a substantial fraction of that gap is a materials gap.

## 2.2 million predictions, 736 confirmations

Body: GNoME funnel — 2.2M crystals predicted, 380K computed stable, only 736 independently synthesized by late 2023 = 0.2% validation rate. This is not a GNoME failure; it is a structural pipeline failure.

## The A-Lab cautionary tale

Body: 63% success headline, then the critique — two-thirds of "novel" targets were known disordered phases; true novel discovery rate collapsed to near zero.

## Four structural barriers

Body: Briefly introduce defect/bulk asymmetry, combinatorial wall, metastability, and ranking inversion as the subject of the rest of the series.

## Why the window is narrow

Body: Deployment decisions made between 2025 and 2035 will lock in material choices for decades; a material discovered in 2030 may miss the 2035 window.
