## The discovery pipeline map

Body: Generation → prediction → synthesis → validation; where MatterGen, GNoME, CHGNet, MACE, Materials Project, A-Lab, and national labs fit; how each layer produces errors that propagate downstream.

## Pillar 1: measured field, not learned

Body: The error field is a physical quantity fixed by three anchor observables; transfers blind across properties within a crystal family; no per-system retraining like delta-ML, no curated data like fine-tuning.

## Pillar 2: machine-checked proof

Body: 77 build-locked Lean 4 theorems with zero sorry proofs; the categorical difference between statistical confidence and mechanical certainty; impossibility proofs as a feature, not a failure.

## Pillar 3: runtime compatibility

Body: EAM-embedding-shaped overlay pair style for LAMMPS; works with CHGNet, MACE, and future uMLIPs without retraining; 15.6% overhead today, <1% tomorrow.

## The moat deepens with use

Body: Each screening campaign adds validated field measurements; each theorem extends the verified domain; each experimental validation tightens the feedback loop. Data-scale moats erode; proof-grade correction coverage compounds.

## The economic case

Body: NIST MGI estimate of $123B–$270B annual value from improved materials innovation infrastructure; ARPA-E's $3.5B catalyzing $11.8B in follow-on funding; Lupine's $3.2M 36-month budget against the value at stake.

## Honesty about what is still open

Body: Field extension to bcc, hcp, and layered structures is Phase 0 work; second-shell corrections are the v2 extension; industrial validation is ongoing. State plainly what is proven and what is not.

## The vision

Body: The trust layer for a real-world Replicator — a world where AI-designed matter can be made, measured, and believed, within the 2025–2035 climate window.
