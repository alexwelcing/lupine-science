## How the targets were chosen

Body: Three criteria — climate impact, discovery difficulty, and a precise Lupine mechanism — and why each target maps a known structural failure mode onto a correction or verification function.

## Batteries: cobalt-free LMR cathodes

Body: 2–5 GtCO₂ avoided; target >300 Wh/kg, >1,000 cycles, <$80/kWh; key barriers are voltage fade, TM migration, and oxygen redox; Lupine mechanism is corrected migration-barrier ranking across 10⁶ compositions.

## Batteries: earth-abundant halide solid electrolytes

Body: 1–3 GtCO₂ avoided; market $886M → $24.3B by 2034; target >10 mS/cm conductivity using Zr/Fe/Al/Mg instead of In/Sc/Y; Lupine mechanism is corrected Li⁺ hop barriers reducing false-negative rate ~50×.

## Carbon removal: MOFs for direct air capture

Body: 0.5–2 GtCO₂/year removal; target >2 mmol/g working capacity at 400 ppm, humidity-stable over >10,000 cycles, <$50/tCO₂; Lupine mechanism is corrected hydrolysis barriers and impossibility proofs for unsupported frameworks.

## Industrial decarbonization: electrochemical ammonia catalysts

Body: ~0.45 GtCO₂/year displaced; Haber-Bosch emits >450 MtCO₂/year; target >60% energy efficiency and >300 mA/cm²; Lupine mechanism is corrected N₂ dissociation barriers and selective field failure for scaling-relation breakers.

## Solar: lead-free perovskite absorbers

Body: 0.5–1 GtCO₂/year deployment acceleration; target >20% certified PCE and >25-year stability; Lupine mechanism is corrected Sn vacancy formation energies and provable boundaries for metastable phases.

## The portfolio view

Body: Combined 5–12 GtCO₂/year and >$650B addressable market; why these targets share defect-dominated properties in multi-component spaces; predictions are necessary but not sufficient — the next step is partners.
