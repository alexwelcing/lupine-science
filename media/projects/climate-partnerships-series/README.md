# Climate Partnerships Series — Project Brief

> A 5-article editorial series translating Lupine Science's climate discovery plan and partnership strategy into a public, investor- and lab-facing narrative.

---

## Project overview

This series turns two internal strategic documents into a publishable story arc:

- `/home/alex/Dev/lupine/climate.md` — the technical discovery plan covering the 0.2% synthesis problem, the environment error field, and five climate-critical material targets.
- `/home/alex/Dev/lupine/partnerships.md` — the strategic partner map for each target, including tier-1 labs, consortia, and industrial collaborators.

The arc moves from problem diagnosis (Articles 1–2) to concrete targets and partners (Articles 3–4) to the strategic moat (Article 5). Every article cites verifiable numbers from the source files and uses Lupine Science's proof-first voice.

---

## Audience

Primary:

- Climate-tech investors evaluating the defensibility of AI-for-materials bets.
- Materials scientists, battery/catalysis/DAC researchers, and national-lab program managers who may become partners or co-authors.
- AI-for-science teams comparing Lupine's correction-layer approach to raw uMLIP or generative-model pipelines.

Secondary:

- Policy and grant-program readers (ARPA-E, DOE, Breakthrough Energy) who need a concise, evidence-based narrative.
- The curious public interested in why the energy transition is stalled by materials, not just money.

## Success criteria

1. **Factual integrity.** Every quantitative claim in every article maps back to a specific passage in `climate.md` or `partnerships.md` and is listed in the proof-pack for the piece.
2. **Narrative coherence.** The five articles read as a single arc: the problem is diagnosed, the method is introduced, the targets are shown, the path to market is grounded in named partners, and the strategic position is defended.
3. **Brand consistency.** All hero assets use the paper/indigo/ink palette and the approved Lupine motifs; no flowers, people, baked-in text, neon, or generic AI tropes.
4. **Production readiness.** Each article placeholder is replaced by a complete draft of 1,500–2,500 words; each has a hero asset, a social card, and a proof-pack.
5. **Cross-cutting deliverables.** Series landing page, sitemap update, social thread, and proof-pack formatting are completed before the first article is published.
6. **Launch gate.** All five articles pass internal review and build/publish checks before any public release.
