# Cement, Concrete, and the Weight of the Built World

> **Type:** article
>
> **Date:** 2026-08-29
>
> **Scope:** Low-CO₂ binders, alternative clinkers, and CO₂-cured concrete; closing argument for the environmental expansion series.
>
> **Description:** Cement emits ~8% of global CO₂. Decarbonizing it requires predicting amorphous, metastable, and carbonated phases — exactly where corrected uMLIPs and formal verification can help.
>
> **Audience:** Materials scientists, construction-industry R&D, climate investors, general readers
>
> **Status:** Draft

<p class="lead">Concrete is the most used material on Earth after water, and its carbon footprint is larger than aviation. Most of those emissions come from calcining limestone — a process that clean electricity alone cannot fix. The answer is new materials, and the new materials are stuck in the same prediction trap.</p>
