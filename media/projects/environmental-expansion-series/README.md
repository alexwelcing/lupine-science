# Environmental Expansion Series — Project Brief

> A 4–5 article editorial series extending Lupine Science's correction-and-verification method from climate-critical materials to environmental challenges beyond CO₂: water scarcity, air quality, methane emissions, refrigerants, critical-mineral recycling, PFAS remediation, and low-carbon cement.

---

## Project overview

This series applies the trust-layer thesis established in the climate-partnerships series to a broader set of planetary boundaries. The premise is that the same structural failure modes — defect/bulk asymmetry, combinatorial explosion, metastability, and ranking inversion — corrupt predictions for materials that matter for water, air, industrial chemistry, and waste. Lupine's environment error field, runtime correction, and formal verification map directly onto these new targets.

The series builds on:

- `/home/alex/Dev/lupine/climate.md` — the technical discovery plan for the environment error field and its climate targets.
- `/home/alex/Dev/lupine/partnerships.md` — the partnership strategy and tier system.
- `/home/alex/Dev/lupine/lupine-science/media/projects/environmental-expansion-series/environment.md` — the source document mapping Lupine's method to non-CO₂ environmental targets (this series' primary technical source).

The narrative arc moves from the principle (error geometry transfers across the periodic table) to the targets (water, air, methane, refrigerants, minerals, PFAS, cement) to the partnership and verification implications.

## Success criteria

1. **Scientific defensibility.** Every material target, barrier, and impact estimate in `environment.md` maps to a published literature source or an established engineering benchmark.
2. **Methodological coherence.** Each target explicitly links a failure mode (defect/bulk asymmetry, combinatorial wall, metastability, ranking inversion) to a Lupine correction or verification function.
3. **Quantified impact.** Articles use defensible ranges (Gt, Mt, billion-dollar market size, percentage reductions) and distinguish avoided emissions from removed pollutants from recovered materials.
4. **Brand consistency.** Hero assets use the paper/indigo/ink palette and approved Lupine motifs; no flowers, people, baked-in text, neon, or generic AI tropes.
5. **Production readiness.** Each article placeholder is replaced by a complete draft of 1,800–2,800 words; each has a hero asset, a social card, and a proof-pack.
6. **Launch gate.** All articles pass internal review and build/publish checks before any public release.
