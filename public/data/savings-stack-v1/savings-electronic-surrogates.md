# Compute-Savings Digest: Electronic-Structure Surrogates that Skip or Shorten the SCF Loop

*Evidence cut: 2026-07-21. Every number below was read this session from the cited source; each entry is marked **[FT]** = full text read, **[ABS]** = abstract only, **[SNIP]** = verified via indexed abstract/snippet mirror. Paywalled journals were accessed via arXiv versions or abstract mirrors.*

## 1. Executive result

- **Machine-learned Hamiltonians (DeepH line, THU; HamGNN, Fudan) eliminate 100% of SCF iterations at inference**, replacing them with O(N) graph-network construction of the Hamiltonian matrix plus one diagonalization. Measured: **10³× wall-time reduction on a MoS₂ 35×35 supercell** (DeepH, [FT]), and a **4,284-atom Si-dislocation Hamiltonian built in 36 s on 80 CPU cores** (HamGNN, [FT]). Accuracy retained: meV-scale Hamiltonian MAE (0.12–3 meV), band structures and wavefunction-derived properties (optics, Berry-phase responses) essentially on top of DFT. This is the most mature "skip the SCF" technology, with independent implementations and shipping packages (DeepH-pack, ABACUS/HONPAS/OpenMX interfaces).
- **Semi-empirical+ML hybrids (OrbNet Denali, AIQM1) deliver the largest verified *chemistry* savings with error bars**: AIQM1 optimized C₆₀ in **14 s on 1 core vs 31 min on 32 cores at ωB97X-D4/def2-TZVPP (~4×10³× CPU-time)** at 0.8 kcal/mol MAD vs CCSD(T)\*/CBS [FT]; OrbNet Denali claims up to 10³× vs DFT with GMTKN55 WTMAD-2 = 9.84 kcal/mol [ABS]. These keep an SCF (a cheap NDDO/xTB one) — they *shorten*, not skip.
- **ML kinetic-energy functionals finally made orbital-free DFT work for molecules**: M-OFDFT (Microsoft Research AI4Science, Beijing/Shanghai) reaches chemical accuracy on QM9/ethanol and a **27.4× speedup on a 738-atom protein (0.45 h vs 12.3 h KSDFT)**, with empirical scaling O(N^1.46) vs O(N^2.49) [FT]. For solids, NN-KEDFs (Imoto PRR 2021, O(N) on SiC [ABS]; Sun–Chen MPN-KEDF in ABACUS for 59 alloys [ABS]) are real but lower-profile.
- **ML exchange-correlation functionals buy accuracy per SCF-dollar, not SCF elimination**: Skala (Microsoft) hits **WTMAD-2 = 2.8 kcal/mol on GMTKN55 — better than all hybrids at semi-local cost** [ABS]; CIDER delivers hybrid-level thermochemistry/band gaps "at roughly the cost of semilocal functionals, significantly faster than hybrid DFT in plane-wave codes" [ABS]; DeePKS gives HSE06-level band gaps of halide perovskites at GGA-like cost [ABS]. The cautionary tale: DM21 (Science 2021) fails SCF convergence on transition-metal complexes and oscillates in geometry optimization (independent 2024–2025 follow-ups).
- **Density prediction shortens rather than skips**: ChargE3Net's predicted-density initialization cut **26.7% of SCF iterations** on unseen Materials-Project crystals [ABS]; MALA's LDOS surrogate gives up to **10³× vs tractable DFT** and enabled >10,000-atom Be electronic structure [ABS]. This "warm-start" mode is the least glamorous but most directly composable saving in the whole subdomain.

## 3. Proven vs claimed

**Replicated and trusted.**
- The **DeepH architecture line** is the most independently corroborated: THU's own series (2022 → DeepH-E3 → DeepH-2 → DeepH-Zero, the variational/unsupervised variant, [arXiv:2403.11287v3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.11287) [ABS]) is now embedded in ABACUS, OpenMX, SIESTA, FHI-aims and HONPAS (10⁴-atom hybrid-functional study, Digital Discovery 2025, cited in DeepH-pack [SNIP](https://arxiv.org/html/2601.02938v1)); third parties publish DeepH-E3 applications (e.g., GaAs defect thermal behavior, [arXiv:2511.18398](https://arxiv.org/html/2511.18398v1) [SNIP]). Accuracy numbers (meV-scale) have been reproduced by competitors (HamGNN's Table 1 cross-compares DeepH and PhiSNet on identical splits, [FT]).
- **AIQM1** has independent re-benchmarks (e.g., barrier heights, J. Chem. Phys. 158, 074103 (2023), seen in citation record [SNIP]) and ships in MLatom/Sparrow. **OrbNet Denali**'s GMTKN55 numbers are on public benchmarks. **CIDER** functionals are released (CiderPressLite) and used outside the Kozinsky group.
- **ML density prediction for warm dense matter** (MALA line) is a multi-institution, multi-paper program with open software.

**Single-paper / single-group claims.**
- **M-OFDFT**'s 27.4× protein speedup and size-extrapolation are internally well-documented but un-replicated; same for **Skala**'s 2.8 kcal/mol GMTKN55 (both from Microsoft Research AI4Science — technically excellent, institutionally concentrated).
- **Universal Hamiltonian models** (universal HamGNN; universal DeepH, [arXiv:2406.10536v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.10536) [ABS]) show Materials-Project-scale training, but universal-model accuracy is visibly below per-system models and no third party has stress-tested them.
- **ChargE3Net**'s 26.7% SCF-iteration cut is a single-group MIT LL result (albeit a concrete, reproducible metric).
- **QHNet/TraceGrad-class** architecture papers report efficiency on their own splits only.

**Marketing / caution flags.**
- "Orders of magnitude" Hamiltonian-surrogate speedups always compare *inference* against full SCF DFT, quietly amortizing the 10²–10³ DFT calculations needed for training data. Fair framing: savings exist when many structures/properties are computed per chemistry.
- **DM21** is the canonical lesson: Science-level headline (GMTKN55 near double-hybrid quality), then independent work found SCF-convergence failures on ~transition-metal chemistry (Zhao et al., PCCP 2024) and oscillatory XC potentials breaking geometry optimization (Ryabov, 2025). Any NN-functional without convergence guarantees should be treated as unproven for production.
- Orbital-free ML-KEDFs before M-OFDFT (2017–2022 wave: Brockherde, Ryczko, Meyer) were honestly framed proofs of concept; claims of generality in press coverage exceeded the papers.

**Honest scope note.** The subdomain is *not* thin — it is one of the densest in computational chemistry — but the "fraction of DFT wall-time eliminated" is only crisply measured for Hamiltonian surrogates (10³× inference), semi-empirical+ML hybrids (10³–10⁴×), M-OFDFT (27× at protein scale), and MALA (10³×). ML-XC papers mostly measure *accuracy-per-rung*, not wall time; their speed claims are qualitative ("significantly faster than hybrid"). ## 4. Openings for Lupine

1. **Hamiltonian surrogates don't give forces; Lupine does.** DeepH/HamGNN produce band structures and response properties, but total energies/forces from a predicted Hamiltonian are not variationally reliable for MD or NEB — which is exactly Lupine's home turf (uMLIP + theorem-gated sparse-anchor DFT). The clean combination: use a DeepH/HamGNN-predicted Hamiltonian (or ChargE3Net-density: measured 26.7% SCF-iteration cut) as the **SCF warm start at each sparse anchor**. Union anchors already share DFT evaluations across models; adding surrogate warm starts compounds 72.4% fewer DFT calls with ~25%+ fewer iterations per call. Nobody has benchmarked "surrogate warm start × sparse-anchor NEB" — an obvious, cheap win.
2. **Theorem-gating is the missing piece for ML functionals.** DeePKS/CIDER/Skala raise accuracy per SCF dollar but are trusted blindly inside their SCF; DM21 showed NN functionals fail catastrophically and silently (TMC convergence, geometry oscillations). Lupine's physical-law theorem checks (e.g., fractional-charge/spin consistency, size-consistency, virial/force–energy consistency along a path) are precisely the runtime certification these functionals lack. A "Skala-inside, Lupine-gated, sparse-anchored" stack targets hybrid-level accuracy at semilocal cost *with* formal failure detection — a stronger story than any of the three alone.
3. **Density mismatch as a union-anchor detector.** Union anchors currently key on agreement of model-predicted extrema in energy space. MALA/Rackers-class density surrogates offer a cheap electronic-level discriminator: where two uMLIPs agree geometrically but predicted densities/Hamiltonians diverge beyond a theorem-set tolerance, promote that structure to a DFT anchor. This converts union anchoring from geometry-driven to physics-driven, and the surrogates cost milliseconds.
4. **Orbital-free partitioning at the large end.** M-OFDFT's verified regime (light elements, near-equilibrium, neutral; 27× at ~700 atoms) is complementary to Lupine's verified regime (reactive paths, barriers). For very large systems (biomolecular, electrochemical interfaces), an OF-surrogate background + Lupine-corrected uMLIP reactive region, with anchors placed where theorems flag the boundary, attacks the regime where even 10³× Hamiltonian surrogates fail (they need converged reference data at the target chemistry, which Lupine's sparse anchors generate on the fly).
