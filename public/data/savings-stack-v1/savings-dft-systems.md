# Chapter digest: Systems-level DFT acceleration (LS-DFT, GPU ports, mixed precision / reduced-rank, SCF accelerators, k-point economization, wfn/density reuse, ML guesses)

Evidence cut: 2026-07-21. Every number below was read this session; each citation is tagged **[full text]** or **[abstract only]**. Items I could not verify are marked [UNVERIFIED].

## 1. Executive result

- **GPU ports give 3–20× per-node wall-clock speedups, but only above a size threshold.** Quantum ESPRESSO-GPU (1×V100 vs 18-core Xeon): 1.4× at 98 atoms/246 electrons, rising to >3× as size grows [full text, [Giannozzi et al., JCP 152, 154105 (2020)](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2104.10502)]. At the engineered end, DFT-FE reports ~20× CPU→GPU node speedup and 80–140 s ground states for 6k–15k-electron systems [abstract, [Das et al., arXiv:2203.07820v2](https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.07820)]; GPU-SPARC hybrid functionals: up to 8× node-hours / 80× core-hours, ~300 s for a >6,000-electron metallic system [abstract, [Jing et al., arXiv:2501.16572](https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.16572)]. GPU speedups are workload- and size-dependent, not flat marketing numbers.
- **Reduced-rank exchange (ACE, ACE-ISDF, + mixed precision) is the biggest algorithmic win for hybrid DFT: ~2 orders of magnitude cheaper exact exchange.** Poisson solves O(N_e²)→O(N_e); a converged HSE calculation on 1,000-atom bulk Si in 10 min on 2,000 cores [abstract, [Hu, Lin, Yang, arXiv:1707.09141](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.09141)]; per-SCF-iteration cost only "marginally larger than GGA" [abstract, [Lin, ACE, arXiv:1601.07159](https://arxiv.org/abs/1601.07159)]. Dual-grid + single-precision ISDF/ACE/FFT adds a further "several-fold" (≈2× for the FFT-heavy part) at acceptable accuracy cost; 8,000-atom Si hybrid on 16,000 CPU cores [abstract, [Hou et al., JCTC 21, 787 (2025), DOI 10.1021/acs.jctc.4c01541](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=Dual-Grid+and+Mixed-Precision+Methods+for+Accelerating+Hybrid+Density+Functional)].
- **Linear-scaling DFT changes the feasible system size, not the speed at fixed size — and it has a real crossover.** CONQUEST: thousands of atoms by diagonalization, millions by LS [full text, [Nakata et al., JCP 152, 164112 (2020)](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2002.07704)]; on an 8-atom cell its LS solver is ~2× *slower* than full TZTP diagonalization — LS only pays at scale. ONETEP/BigDFT/CP2K report thousands of atoms at PW accuracy with LS effort [abstracts: [Prentice et al., DOI 10.1063/5.0004445](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32384832/); [Ratcliff et al., DOI 10.1063/5.0004792](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33687268/); [Kühne et al., arXiv:2003.03868](https://arxiv.org/abs/2003.03868)].
- **SCF-iteration economics is the cheapest, most portable lever: 3× fewer iterations on hard metals, elimination of per-step SCF in AIMD, and 20–33% iteration cuts from ML initial guesses.** Periodic Pulay: 3× fewer iterations than DIIS on Pd-bulk, converges at 25 K where Pulay fails [full text, [Banerjee et al., CPL 647, 31 (2016)](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1512.01604)]. XL-BOMD removes the iterative ground-state optimization per MD step with no systematic energy drift [abstract, [Niklasson, JCP 147, 054103 (2017)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.10845)]. ML density guesses: −33.3% SCF iterations transferable to 900-atom systems [abstract, [Liu et al. (ByteDance), arXiv:2509.25724v3](https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.25724)]; −~20% *total* DFT cost for periodic materials, the first end-to-end-positive result — only because inference is 633× faster than prior density models [full text, [Ærtebjerg et al., ELECTRAFI, arXiv:2601.19966v2](https://arxiv.org/html/2601.19966v2)].
- **k-point economization is real but underused: 60% fewer k-points for metals at 1 meV/atom** using generalized regular grids vs Monkhorst-Pack [abstract, [Morgan et al., Comput. Mater. Sci. 153, 424 (2018)](https://arxiv.org/abs/1804.04741)]. Smearing economization has essentially no quantified 2015–2026 literature — it's practitioner folklore (honest gap).

## 3. Proven vs claimed

**Replicated and trusted**
- GPU acceleration of PW/PAW DFT at 2–20× per node is confirmed independently across QE [full text], VASP [full text], FHI-aims, ABACUS, SPARC, DFT-FE, TeraChem-lineage — with consistent lessons: gains grow with system size, small cells (<~100 atoms) can see ~1×, and GPU memory is the binding constraint. The honest metric is node-hours (SPARC's 8× node-hours vs 80× core-hours framing shows how raw "×" inflates if you count cores).
- ACE/ISDF reduced-rank exchange: implemented in multiple codes (PWDFT, SPARC, others) with follow-on papers (noncollinear spin, RT-TDDFT, mixed precision, MP2) — the ~2-order-of-magnitude exchange-cost reduction for thousand-atom hybrids is credible and replicated in use.
- Periodic Pulay: full-text verified 2–3× iteration reduction, independently observed in a second code (ClusterES), and adopted as default-style mixing in SPARC production runs (per the SPARC hybrid GPU paper).
- XL-BOMD / CP2G density-matrix propagation: decade-long literature lineage; the "no per-step iterative SCF" property is in the abstract of the 2017 JCP paper; the 2026 CP2G tutorial confirms it survives contact with real production settings (with tuning caveats).

**Single-paper / single-group claims**
- ELECTRAFI's −20% end-to-end DFT cost for materials (2026 preprint, one group, ICML-style submission). The mechanism (inference cost eats SCF savings) is convincing, but it needs independent reproduction — especially since prior density models reportedly *increased* total time.
- ByteDance's −33.3% SCF iterations: one group, molecular Gaussian-basis only; the accompanying failure of Hamiltonian-based baselines (+80% or divergence) is a useful warning, but external validation is absent.
- GR k-point grids: one group (BYU), but 7,000+ structure tests and a simple, checkable algorithm; still under-adopted by major codes' default workflows.
- Hou dual-grid/mixed-precision: one group (Lin Lin's collaboration), peer-reviewed (JCTC 2025); "several times" is abstract-level language — treat the precise factor as system-dependent.

**Marketing / non-citable**
- PWmat ">30× GPU speedup" appears only in vendor blogs/press; the peer-reviewed PWmat GPU papers (Jia et al., CPC 211, 8 (2017)) were not abstract-accessible this session → treat all PWmat numbers as marketing until the CPC paper is read.
- Generic "GPUs give 10–100× DFT speedups" blog claims (e.g., mqs.dk): not citable, contradicted by the measured 1.4–3× QE numbers at realistic sizes.
- PWDFT-SW 64.8×: real but Sunway-specific, and I only accessed it second-hand via the ABACUS paper.

**Thin sub-areas (honest assessment)**: ONETEP has no dedicated peer-reviewed GPU-port paper in 2015–2026 that I could find; k-point *smearing* economization and wavefunction reuse *along NEB bands* have no quantified literature — both are practitioner knowledge, marked [UNVERIFIED].

## 4. Openings for Lupine

- **Multiplicative stacking is the headline: sparse/union anchors sit on top of every technique here.** GPU ports, LS-DFT, and ACE-ISDF cut the cost *per DFT evaluation*; sparse anchors (4–6 images near the saddle) cut the *number of evaluations* ~4–8× vs a dense band, and union anchors cut it again 3.62× across models (our 154 vs 558 measurement). These are orthogonal multipliers: an ACE-ISDF-class hybrid anchor (~10 min for 1,000-atom Si on 2,000 cores, [Hu et al. 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1707.09141)) × sparse-anchor sampling makes *hybrid-quality barrier libraries* feasible where a dense hybrid NEB is not. Nobody in this literature combines reduced-rank hybrid DFT with reduced-image path sampling — that's a publishable gap.
- **Wavefunction/density reuse along the band is an unclaimed, unquantified win.** The MD world formalized this (XL-BOMD removes per-step SCF entirely, [Niklasson 2017](https://arxiv.org/abs/1705.10845)); the NEB world has zero quantified studies. Because sparse anchors concentrate DFT images in a small configurational region near the saddle, image-to-image density extrapolation (ASPC-style, or ELECTRAFI-style ML density guess at each anchor, −20% total cost, [arXiv:2601.19966](https://arxiv.org/html/2601.19966v2)) should be *more* effective there than across a whole band — the images are closer together. Lupine can define, measure, and Lean-certify the error bound for density extrapolation across neighboring anchor images; this directly compounds with our 72.4% union-anchor saving.
- **Theorem gates as a robustness layer over aggressive accelerators.** The measured failure modes in this subdomain are exactly where gates help: classical Pulay *diverges* on metals at low T where Periodic Pulay still converges ([Banerjee 2016](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/1512.01604)); mixed-precision and dual-grid introduce explicit, bounded accuracy trade-offs ([Hou 2025](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/?term=Dual-Grid+and+Mixed-Precision+Methods+for+Accelerating+Hybrid+Density+Functional)); ADMM is an approximation to the exchange energy ([Kühne 2020](https://ar5iv.labs.arxiv.org/html/2003.03868)). A theorem-gated runtime corrector can certify per-anchor convergence (residual bounds, force consistency between uMLIP prediction and DFT anchor) and only then accept the cheap settings — turning "fast but fragile" defaults (single-precision HFX, loose mixing) into gated defaults with verified fallback to DP/standard settings. That is a trust argument no GPU or mixing paper can make.
- **Union anchors exploit what these techniques cannot: cross-model agreement.** All techniques above accelerate one model's DFT. Our measured 154 shared vs 558 naive evaluations (72.4% fewer; 3.62×) comes from uMLIP extremum agreement — a structural saving independent of hardware or algebra. The natural endpoint: uMLIP-predicted saddle region → union-shared sparse anchors → each anchor evaluated with GPU + ACE-ISDF + mixed-precision DFT, started from an ML density guess. Estimated stacking from verified numbers: 3.62× (union) × 3–8× (sparse vs dense) × ~2× (mixed precision, [Hou 2025]) × ~1.25× (density guess, [ELECTRAFI]) ≈ 27–72× total reduction in barrier-library DFT cost relative to naive dense double-precision CPU NEB — every factor individually cited above, the product itself being Lupine's contribution to demonstrate.
