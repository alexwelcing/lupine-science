# Chapter Digest: Active Learning for Interatomic Potentials as a DFT-Call Economizer

**Scope note.** This subdomain is *not* thin — it is one of the most densely evidenced compute-savings areas in atomistic simulation. Every number below was read this session from the cited arXiv abstract page or full text (marked accordingly); journal-only items were cross-checked via Crossref/ACS metadata. Evidence cut: 2026-07-21.

## 1. Executive result

- **Uncertainty/committee-triggered labeling reduces DFT single-points by 2–4 orders of magnitude versus dense sampling, and this is replicated across five independent ecosystems.** VASP's on-the-fly Bayesian MLFF bypasses ">99% of the first-principles calculations" during force-field generation ([Jinnouchi, Karsai, Kresse, PRB 100, 014105 (2019), arXiv:1904.12961v3, abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.12961)); DP-GEN labeled only 0.0044% of ~650M explored configurations for the Al–Mg alloy potential ([Zhang et al., PRM 3, 023804 (2019), arXiv:1810.11890v2, full text](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.11890)); FLARE trains usable force fields with "~100 DFT calculations" per system ([Vandermause et al., npj Comput. Mater. 6, 20 (2020), arXiv:1904.02042v3, full text](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.02042)).
- **The total DFT bill for a general-purpose single-element potential is now ~10²–10⁴ single-points, depending on accuracy target and phase-space coverage.** ~100 calls for one-phase FLARE models; 7,646 labels (0.03% of 25M explored) for the general Cu DP-GEN potential covering 50 K–2T_m and 1–50,000 bar ([Zhang et al., CPC 253, 107206 (2020), arXiv:1910.12690v1, full text](https://arxiv.org/abs/1910.12690)); 814 reference calculations for a committee water model spanning liquid, ice phases, and the air–water interface *including* nuclear quantum effects ([Schran et al., JCP 153, 104105 (2020), arXiv:2006.01541v2, abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2006.01541)).
- **Query-by-committee delivers the cleanest measured data-reduction factor at fixed accuracy:** ANI active learning matched ANI-1 on the COMP6 benchmark with **10% of the data** and "vastly outperformed" it with 25% — an order-of-magnitude economization against naive/random sampling ([Smith et al., JCP 148, 241733 (2018), arXiv:1801.09319v2, abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/1801.09319)).
- **Rare events are the documented weak point of all mainstream triggers.** DP-GEN's own authors state the committee indicator is "only a sufficient, not a necessary, condition for poor performance" (ensembles can agree and be wrong) (full text, arXiv:1810.11890); FLARE's GP epistemic uncertainty saturates and *underestimates* true error under large extrapolation (δ>20% distortion) (full text, arXiv:1904.02042); standard MD-driven AL "is prone to miss either rare events or extrapolative regions" ([Zaverkin et al., npj Comput. Mater. 10, 83 (2024), arXiv:2312.01416v2, abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.01416)).
- **The savings frontier in 2025–2026 has shifted to (a) shrinking the cell, not just the count — small-cell AL gives up to 100× cost reduction ([Meng et al., arXiv:2504.07293v1, abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.07293)) — and (b) on-the-fly fine-tuning of foundation models ([Rensmeyer et al., Digital Discovery (2026), DOI 10.1039/D5DD00392J, abstract](https://pubs.rsc.org/en/content/articlelanding/2026/dd/d5dd00392j))** — i.e., the field is converging on exactly Lupine's design space.

## 3. Proven vs claimed

**Replicated and trusted (multi-group, multi-codebase):**
- Uncertainty/committee-triggered labeling cuts DFT single-points by ~10×–1000× versus dense or random sampling at matched accuracy. Independently demonstrated by the ANI line (10% of data at parity, LANL/UNC), DP-GEN line (0.0044–0.03% label rates, Princeton/IAPCM/DeepModeling), VASP-MLFF line (>99% bypass, Vienna/Toyota), FLARE line (~100 calls/system, Harvard/Bosch), and MTP line (Skoltech). These are production workflows with thousands of citations and open-source implementations (DeePMD-kit/DP-GEN, VASP≥6.3, FLARE, MLIP-3, ASE calculators).
- GP/Bayesian internal uncertainties track true error well *near* the training set (FLARE Fig. 2a–c; VASP Bayesian error) — sufficient as *triggers*, not as *guarantees*.
- Iterative database extension (GAP line, Cambridge) is the trusted manual ancestor of automated AL and documented its own early failure (sub-Å clumping) before hierarchical descriptors fixed it.

**Single-paper or thin-replication claims:**
- FLARE's "~100 DFT calls" per system: now partially corroborated by FALCON (151 calls for H₂O/CNT; 569 for Al melting over 250 ps), so graduating toward proven — but both are GPR-family, and FALCON itself warns retraining can eat the savings for large/hot systems.
- Small-cell AL 100× (one 2025 paper, alkali metals only).
- Uncertainty-biased MD and Bayesian fine-tuning of foundation models (2024/2026, abstracts only here; no independent replication yet).
- Per-environment AL (MLIP-3) and ACE MaxVol-vs-ensemble equivalence: methods papers without hard per-system DFT-call factors.

**Marketing / handle-with-care:**
- "Orders of magnitude" in perspective pieces (JPCL 2020 included) never define the baseline; the honest unit is *DFT single-points to reach a stated force RMSE on a stated validation set*, and almost no two papers use the same one.
- Reported label fractions (0.0044%) ignore exploration cost — cheap per step but run over 10⁷–10⁸ configurations, and ignore the 4× ensemble training overhead.
- Threshold sensitivity is real: FALCON's own numbers show a 4× swing in DFT calls between accuracy thresholds of 0.10 and 0.20 eV. Any "DFT calls needed" figure without its threshold is not comparable across papers.
- The a-C total training-set size is widely quoted second-hand but is not in the main text [UNVERIFIED — stated only in the paper's SI, not accessed].

## 4. Openings for Lupine

1. **Theorem-gating fills the documented "confident-but-wrong" blind spot of every learned-UQ trigger.** DP-GEN's authors concede the committee indicator is sufficient-but-not-necessary (ensemble can agree and be wrong, arXiv:1810.11890 full text); FLARE's GP variance saturates and *underestimates* error under strong extrapolation (arXiv:1904.02042 full text); Tan et al. benchmarked that no UQ scheme consistently flags OOD ([npj Comput. Mater. 2023, arXiv:2305.01754v1, abstract](https://arxiv.org/abs/2305.01754)). A Lean-formalized physical-law check (symmetry, conservation, curvature-sign at saddles) is an independent, non-learned oracle that can veto predictions exactly where committees and GP variances are structurally blind — Lupine should frame theorem-gating as the *necessary-condition* complement to AL's sufficient-condition triggers.
2. **Union anchors are an orthogonal savings axis no AL framework addresses.** AL economizes labels *within* one model's training; nothing in this literature deduplicates DFT evaluations *across* models. Lupine's measured 154 shared vs 558 naive evaluations (72.4% fewer on a 29-path panel) stacks multiplicatively with AL's per-model savings: run FLARE/DP-GEN-style uncertainty triggers, but only label the union of cross-model disagreement. The same logic extends small-cell AL (Meng et al. 2025, 100×): Lupine's sparse anchors can be evaluated as small cells around the predicted saddle for another ~2 orders of magnitude, turning the AL "label count" game into a "label count × cell volume" game.
3. **Barrier-focused sparse anchoring vs rare-event MD learning.** FLARE's flagship rare-event demonstration (Al vacancy) needed a 1 ns on-the-fly run to learn one migration mechanism; Lupine's 4–6 DFT images near the model-predicted saddle gets the barrier directly. The 2026 foundation-model fine-tuning line (Rensmeyer) independently identifies transition states as the high-value rare events worth oversampling — Lupine can position sparse/union anchoring + theorem-verified saddle geometry as the deterministic, retraining-free answer to the same need.
4. **Replace "label-and-retrain" with "verify-and-patch" to kill AL's documented cost spike.** FALCON's honest accounting shows GPR retraining can dominate and even exceed AIMD cost at tight thresholds or high T (full text). Lupine's runtime correction patches uMLIP errors against physical theorems without retraining the base model — directly attacking the cost component (retraining overhead) that AL papers systematically under-report, while keeping a DFT ledger only for theorem-violating regions.

**Caveats for the chapter editors.** (i) Cross-paper DFT-call comparisons are apples-to-oranges — thresholds, system sizes, and phase-space coverage differ; I flagged context per row. (ii) Two numbers I could not verify are marked [UNVERIFIED] (a-C total database size; Li et al. 2015's factor-30 is verified only as quoted in the Podryabinkin–Shapeev full text). (iii) The MAPbI₃ PRL (arXiv:1903.09613) and stanene npj paper (arXiv:2008.11796) were verified at abstract level only — cited for existence/claims, not for per-system DFT counts. (iv) The Zaverkin (arXiv:2312.01416) "existing methods miss rare events" statement is the field's own most quotable failure admission — useful rhetorically, but it is an abstract-level claim of a 2024 single paper.
