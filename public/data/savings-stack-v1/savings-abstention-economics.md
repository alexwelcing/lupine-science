# Digest: Uncertainty-gated selective DFT and the economics of abstention

Scope note: per instructions, I did **not** re-survey the certified-inference per-step gating literature (coverage/selectivity theory, gate runtime costs). Everything below is economics: measured DFT-call budgets, labeling/abstention fractions, and accuracy-per-call pricing. Access level per source is marked **FT** (full text read this session) or **ABS** (abstract only). Evidence cut: 2026-07-21.

## 1. Executive result

- **The biggest, most replicated saving is abstention during training data generation, not during inference.** Bayesian-error and ensemble-disagreement gates routinely skip 99%+ of first-principles evaluations: VASP's on-the-fly MLFF skips >99% of FP steps during force-field generation (Jinnouchi/Kresse, PRL 2019 **FT**; PRB 2019 **ABS**), and DP-GEN labeled only 0.0044% of ~650 million explored Al/Mg/Al-Mg configurations (Zhang/Car/E, PRM 2019 **FT**). Final models retain near-FP accuracy (2.6 meV/atom, 0.07 eV/Å in the perovskite case).
- **Query-by-committee gates price data at roughly 10%.** Across two independent ecosystems (LANL's ANI and Princeton/IAPCM's DP), ensemble-disagreement selection reaches matched or better accuracy with ~10% of the data of naive sampling: ANI-1x matched ANI-1 on COMP6 with 10% of the data (Smith et al., JCP 2018 **ABS**), and ANI-1ccx used ~480k QbC-selected CCSD(T) points from a 5M pool (~10%) to reach coupled-cluster-level accuracy (Nat. Commun. 2019 **FT** of preprint).
- **Reactive-force-field budgets are now countable in hundreds of calls.** FLARE's SGP-uncertainty gate trained a full reactive H/Pt(111) force field with 250 DFT calls (216 in the reactive run) in ~3 days, then ran 500 ps of two-phase reactive MD reproducing the experimental activation energy (0.25(2) vs 0.21–0.24 eV) (Vandermause et al., npj Comput. Mater. 2022 **FT**). The earlier FLARE paper reports <100 DFT calls per 10 ps on-the-fly run (npj Comput. Mater. 2020 **ABS+snippet**).
- **Uncertainty-gated saddle/path searches save an order of magnitude in oracle evaluations.** GP-regression NEB cuts energy/force evaluations by ~10x (Koistinen/Jónsson, JCP 2017 **ABS**), replicated by an independent 2025 on-the-fly GPR surrogate reporting 3–10x NEB acceleration (GPR_calculator, arXiv:2504.07319 **ABS**). This is the closest published analogue to Lupine's sparse-anchor NEB economics.
- **In production screening, the abstention rate is measured and it is ~13%.** AdsorbML's ML-rank-then-DFT-verify protocol finds the DFT-level adsorption minimum in 87.4% of ~1000 OC20-Dense systems while doing only top-k DFT single-points (~2000x effective speedup; the ~13% "failure" tail is what you still pay full DFT for) (Lan et al., npj Comput. Mater. 2023 **ABS + v3 text**). This is the cleanest published accuracy-per-DFT-call tradeoff curve in the subdomain.

## 3. Proven vs claimed

**Replicated and trusted (multiple independent groups, numbers reproduced):**
- *Ensemble-disagreement (QbC / model-deviation) gating for training-data economics.* Independently built and validated by: LANL (ANI-1x/1ccx, 10%-of-data pricing), Princeton–IAPCM Beijing (DP-GEN, 0.0044% labeling), Harvard MIR (FLARE, hundreds of calls per reactive FF), Cambridge/Ortner-Csányi (HAL, 88-config alloy ACE), Skoltech (MTP maxvol grades, alloy hulls), and Charles Univ. (committee NNPs, Schran 2020). The "~10% of naive data" and ">99% of explored frames skipped" figures are consistent across these — this is the closest thing to a law in this subdomain.
- *Bayesian/GP error gates calling the oracle only on novel environments during MD.* VASP MLFF (>99% skip, many follow-up systems: zirconia, perovskites) and FLARE (<100–250 calls) agree quantitatively.
- *GP-surrogate gating of path/saddle searches.* Jónsson group's 2017–2020 series (order-of-magnitude fewer evaluations) was independently re-implemented with the same 3–10x conclusion by GPR_calculator (2025).
- *Δ-ML economics.* Ramakrishnan/von Lilienfeld 2015 (1–10% labels) is independently echoed at materials scale by the VASP group's RPA-ΔML (168 high-level structures, <150k CPU h) and by ANI-1ccx at molecular scale.

**Single-group or young claims (real numbers, not yet replicated):**
- UDD-AL uncertainty-biased sampling (LANL, 2023) — conceptually mirrored by HAL (Cambridge, 2023, citing it), so the *idea* has two-lineage support, but the glycine/acetylacetone numbers are single-study.
- Annevelink–Viswanathan's statistical cutoff (2023) — important because it *quantifies when ensemble σ fails*; single study, two datasets.
- UBMD calibrated gradient UQ (Zaverkin/Kästner, 2024) — single group, strong systems (MIL-53(Al)).
- Evidential gates (eIP 2024, e²IP 2026; Fudan/DP-Technology lineage) — real AL/UDD demos but gate-economics numbers are qualitative.
- Flexible CP calibration on MACE-MP-0 (2025) — preprint.

**Marketing-adjacent (true but inflated framing):**
- Gubaev's "3–4 orders of magnitude" folds surrogate relaxation speed into the DFT-call story.
- AdsorbML's "2000x" is relative to a Random+Heuristic DFT baseline, not to tuned DFT practice; the honest, robust number is the 87% success / 13% paid-tail measurement.
- "Negligible-overhead UQ" claims (eIP, flexible CP) never price the gate in DFT calls saved — a gap the field leaves open.

**Notably thin (honest assessment):** true *production-MD* abstention rates (post-training, the fraction of deployed inference sent back to DFT) are almost never reported; nearly all published fractions are from training campaigns. AdsorbML's ~13% verify tail and the on-the-fly "calls become infrequent after 3–10 ps" observation (FLARE) are the only production-mode numbers I could verify. Conformal/evidential gates likewise have **no published DFT-call budgets** — they sell calibration, not priced abstention.

## 4. Openings for Lupine

1. **Union anchors are a genuinely unpriced quantity.** Every published budget above is per-model/per-potential (DP-GEN 0.0044%, FLARE 250 calls, zirconia 168 RPA points). No paper measures DFT-call amortization across *multiple architecturally distinct uMLIPs* sharing an oracle. Lupine's 154-vs-558 union-anchor result (72.4% fewer evaluations), as far as I can verify, is the first cross-model sharing economy number in this literature — publishable as the missing "multi-tenant abstention" baseline, directly comparable against the per-model numbers in §2.
2. **Barrier-local selective DFT, priced against GP-NEB.** The only oracle-call economics for saddle searches are GP-surrogate NEB (10x, Jónsson) and its 2025 replication (3–10x). Both retrain a global surrogate during the search; neither *gates* evaluations by physical-law theorems. A Lupine risk-coverage curve in units of "barrier error (meV) vs DFT anchors per path" — with the theorem gate deciding *which* anchor is provably needed — would be the first abstention pricing for NEB and slots next to Koistinen's number as a competitor baseline.
3. **Fix the documented failure mode of ensemble gates with certified accept regions.** Annevelink–Viswanathan (and the 2024 spatially-resolved-uncertainty ChemRxiv study, accessed this session) show raw ensemble σ decorrelates from true error; current gates compensate with hand-tuned thresholds (e.g., Kulichenko's ρ = 0.35 kcal/mol/√N, chosen because the literature default was "too low"). A Lean-formalized theorem gate that certifies the accept region (rather than thresholding a heuristic σ) is exactly the repair this literature is implicitly asking for, and UDD-AL's own text flags automatic threshold selection as an open problem.
4. **Runtime correction converts training-time abstention into production-time abstention.** The measured production gap (§3) exists because FLARE/VASP gates decide "retrain" — their abstention ends when training ends. Lupine's gate decides "correct this prediction now," which is the missing production-mode measurement: an abstention rate for deployed uMLIP MD (fraction of steps/structures corrected by sparse DFT anchors at runtime), with Δ-ML-style budgets (1–10% labels, 168-point high-level sets) as the pricing precedent.
